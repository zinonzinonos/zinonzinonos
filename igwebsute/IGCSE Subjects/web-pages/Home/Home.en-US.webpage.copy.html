The British KS4 Curriculum

The Key Stage 4 (KS4) curriculum forms the foundation of the British education system for students aged 14-16.

It is designed to provide a well-rounded education through a combination of core subjects, such as English, Mathematics, and Sciences, along with a variety of optional subjects. This balance ensures students gain essential knowledge while exploring personal interests and future aspirations.

KS4 Curriculum at Foley's

Core Subjects

The core subjects are compulsory for all students, ensuring they develop critical foundational skills and knowledge essential for future academic and career success:

  • English Language
  • English Literature
  • Mathematics
  • Global Perspectives
  • One Science subject (Biology, Chemistry, or Physics)

Optional Subjects

Students have the opportunity to select additional subjects based on their interests and career aspirations. This allows them to personalize their education while maintaining a broad and balanced curriculum.

Benefits of Studying a Broad Curriculum at KS4

  • ​Diverse Skill Development: Students acquire skills across disciplines, such as scientific reasoning, literary analysis, creativity, and computational thinking.
  • ​Enhanced Career Opportunities: A broad curriculum allows students to keep multiple career paths open, ensuring flexibility in higher education and future job markets.
  • Global Perspective: The IGCSE curriculum often incorporates international contexts, promoting cultural awareness and global citizenship.
  • Improved Problem-Solving: Exposure to varied disciplines enhances students' ability to approach problems from multiple perspectives.
  • Personal Growth: Exploring different subjects fosters curiosity and helps students discover their passions and strengths.
  • Strong Foundation for Further Education: A wide-ranging education equips students with foundational knowledge and skills needed for post-16 qualifications.

By studying a broad range of IGCSEs, students not only meet the academic challenges of KS4 but also develop into well-rounded individuals ready to excel in their future endeavors.

Guidance for Choosing Options

The options process is a guided choice based on negotiation between the school, pupil, and home. The goal is to match available courses to the aptitude and interests of each pupil to maximize success at Key Stage 4.

Selecting the right IGCSE subjects is an important step that can shape your future academic and career pathways. At Foley’s School, we provide support to help students make informed decisions. Here are some key considerations:

Understand Your Strengths and Interests:

Reflect on the subjects you enjoy and excel in. Passion and aptitude in a subject often lead to better performance.

Explore Career Goals:

If you have a specific career or university course in mind, research the subject requirements. For example, Medicine may require Biology and Chemistry, while Engineering often needs Physics and Mathematics.

Maintain a Balanced Curriculum:

Choose a variety of subjects to keep your future options open. A balance between sciences, humanities, and creative subjects ensures a well-rounded education.

Seek Advice:

Talk to teachers, career counselors, and family members for guidance. They can provide valuable insights based on your skills and aspirations.

Review Subject Content and Assessment:

Consider the workload, type of assessments, and skills required for each subject. Ensure you are prepared for the challenges each subject may present.

Important Notes on Option Blocks and Submissions

  • Our options are arranged into six blocks, from which students are required to choose one subject per block.
  • Certain combinations are not allowed to ensure curriculum balance and coherence:
    • Economics and Business
    • ICT and Computer Science
    • Science Single Award with any of the individual Science subjects (Biology, Chemistry, or Physics)
  • Students can review their choices as they progress through the form and will have the option to print their selections before submission.
  • The form allows multiple submissions, but only the last submission will be considered valid.

Recommendations for Selecting Options
